How It Started

Franz Ferdinand was the Archduke of Austria-Hungary. An assassination took place during a visit to Sarajevo when on June 28, 1914 a member of the Back Hand, a group that was anti-austrian and created in Serbia, shot the Archduke and his wife Sophie, Duchess of Hohenburg, at point blank range killing both shortly after.

Military Advancements

New weapons were introduced in WW1 such as fully automatic weapons like machine guns which were devastating in trench warfare. Tanks were also introduced as these armored vehicles that didn't have the canon on top like we are familliar with. These tanks had terrible mobility rendering them almost useless. These vehicles would be perfected in WW2. Lastly there were artilleries which are large caliber canon like weapons that dealt massive damage and eardrum shattering noise.

The Alliances

Two major alliances were formed in the beginning of WW1. The two were called the Triple Alliance and consisted of Germany, Austria-Hungary and Italy. The other Alliance was named The Triple Entente and consisted of France, Great Britain and Russia

Trench Warfare

Trench Warfare, as the name suggests, were battles were taken place on each side of a large area called "No Man's Land" and the soldiers fighting dug up trenches to live in. There are around 500 miles worth of trenches.

Western and Eastern Fronts

The Western front was located in Northern France where many of the major armies such as German and French, fought. The fighting in the war would end in this front. The Eastern front was mainly RUssia defending it's territory from the Germans. This front would also be more brutal because of geographic conditions.

The Terms of Versailles

The Treaty of Versailles was the treaty that ended WW1. It was signed on June 28 1919. The treaty was a peace offering between Germany and the allies. The terms heavily affected Germany by demanding loss of territory, huge payments and loss of a grand military. All this led Germany into poverty which caused Hitler's uprising and his Nazi party.
